Why WildCoders

Safety & compliance

Built for kids. Built for the people responsible for kids.

We know that bringing any AI into a school district is a serious decision. Koda was built with that responsibility in mind — every input filtered, every output age-appropriate, every conversation visible to the adults who need to see it.

  • 🛡️
    Every input filtered. Kids can't drift into territory we'd never allow. Content moderation runs before every model call.
  • 📖
    Every conversation visible. Parents and teachers can review every session. Nothing is hidden from the adults responsible for the kid.
  • 🚫
    No ads. No data sales. Ever. We're funded by subscriptions and school programs — not by selling student data. Your students are users, not products.
  • 📋
    Working toward FERPA & COPPA certification. We follow the data minimization, parental consent, and access standards that K-8 school programs require, and are working toward formal compliance review. We're happy to share our current data practices in detail.
  • 🐯
    A coach, not a companion. Koda is purposefully designed to be a learning coach — not an emotional companion. Sessions have clear beginnings, middles, and ends.

Questions from administrators

What schools ask us.

What grade levels does WildCoders support?
WildCoders is purpose-built for ages 8–13, which roughly maps to grades 3 through 8. Our coaching philosophy and content design are calibrated for this developmental window. We don't recommend WildCoders for kids younger than 8 or for high school students.
How does WildCoders handle student data?
We follow data minimization principles aligned with FERPA and COPPA standards, and are actively working toward formal compliance review. Student data stays in the student's account, isn't sold or shared with third parties, and is only used to improve the experience for that student. We're happy to share our current data practices in detail.
Is the platform aligned to standards?
Standards alignment is in active development. Our roadmap includes Common Core math & ELA mapping, NGSS science alignment, and computational thinking standards. We're rolling these out in partnership with our pilot districts to make sure they reflect what teachers actually need.
How is Koda different from ChatGPT for classrooms?
ChatGPT is a general-purpose tool built for adults. Koda is a kid-safe coach built around clear learning principles — designed for ages 8–13, governed by coaching pillars that emphasize student thinking over answer-giving, and wrapped in safety controls (input filtering, parent visibility, no ads, no data sales) that classroom AI requires.
What does pricing look like for districts?
We work with each pilot district to find pilot terms that make sense for your size, scope, and timeline. After the pilot, district pricing scales with your enrollment and includes per-student licenses, teacher dashboard access, and the school giveback program. Reach out to discuss specifics.
Will Koda do my students' homework for them?
No. Koda is built specifically to ask coaching questions instead of giving answers. When a student types "what's the answer to question 3," Koda asks them what they've tried, what part is confusing, and walks them through their own thinking. Teachers consistently tell us this is the most important difference between Koda and other AI tools.
What technology do schools need to use WildCoders?
WildCoders runs in any modern web browser — Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox. It works on Chromebooks, iPads, Windows laptops, and Macs. No software to install, no special hardware required.
How do parents and teachers stay coordinated?
Both parents and teachers see what students are working on, with parents getting session-by-session reports at home and teachers getting class-level dashboards. The shared visibility means everyone is working from the same evidence — no duplicate effort, no conflicting messages to the kid.
